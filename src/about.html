Reading Books

Book List

The murder of Roger Ackroyd

The famous detective novel "The Murder of Roger Ackroyd" by Agatha Christie was published in 1926. It follows Christie's famous investigator Hercule Poirot as he investigates the murder of rich businessman Roger Ackroyd in a tiny English town. The novel is well recognized for its deft narrative twist, which startled readers when it was initially released and has since grown to become one of the most well-known and divisive endings in mystery fiction history. Even now, the book is still read and regarded as a masterpiece in its area.

Frankenstein

English writer Mary Shelley published her novel Frankenstein, also known as The Modern Prometheus, in 1818. In the unconventional scientific experiment depicted in Frankenstein, a young scientist named Victor Frankenstein produces a sentient monster.

The Silent Patient

The frightening psychological thriller The Silent Patient tells the story of a lady who beats her husband and the therapist who is determined to learn more about it.

Tell Me Your Dreams

The protagonists of this book are Ashley Patterson, a stunning, intelligent, and hardworking techie who works for a prestigious silicon valley company; Toni Prescott, a party animal who enjoys singing and dancing; and Alette Peters, an art enthusiast with a natural ability to paint.

Watching Movies

Movie list

Friends

Friends is a 90s comedy television series set in Manhattan that follows six friends as they go through nearly every stage of life together, including falling in love, getting married, getting divorced, having kids, fighting, changing jobs, losing jobs, and all kinds of drama.

The Big Bang Theory

Two male Caltech scientists who live together in a Pasadena, California apartment are the focus of the episode. Both of them are unmarried and in their twenties.The program then continues to depict their ongoing battle and search for a partner.

Bridgerton

The eight close-knit siblings of the wealthy and influential Bridgerton family—Anthony, Benedict, Colin, Daphne, Eloise, Francesca, Gregory, and Hyacinth—navigate London high society in quest of love against the backdrop of the Regency era.
